Home About
Professional bio

More than two and a half decades of patient care, with 23+ years devoted to IVF.

Dr. Mona Shroff is an MBBS, MD (Obstetrics & Gynaecology) with over 25 years of clinical experience and more than 23 years dedicated exclusively to IVF and reproductive medicine. She currently serves as National Strategic Director and Senior Clinical Director at Nova IVF Fertility, where she helps shape clinical quality, protocol consistency, and patient-centred care across centres.

She is an ex-Professor at SMIMER Medical College, Surat, a Master Trainer in Emergency Obstetric Care under JHPIEGO, and a mentor to hundreds of doctors, embryologists, and IVF teams. Her philosophy remains constant: combine science, honesty, and empathy so each couple can make informed decisions with dignity.

  • National Strategic Director and Senior Clinical Director, Nova IVF Fertility
  • Ex-Professor, SMIMER Medical College, Surat

Core Focus

The work she is most trusted for

Dr. Mona's clinical work focuses on improving IVF outcomes while reducing confusion, emotional overload, and unnecessary escalation in fertility treatment.

Advanced reproductive medicine

IVF and ICSI planning

Protocol design and cycle management grounded in evidence, reproductive biology, and realistic patient-specific optimisation.

Complex fertility journeys

Repeated IVF failure and implantation failure

Reviewing stimulation, embryo quality, endometrial receptivity, and clinical sequencing to identify what needs to change next.

Condition-specific infertility

Endometriosis, PCOS, and age-related infertility

Care pathways tailored to inflammatory disease, ovulatory dysfunction, ovarian reserve realities, and timing-sensitive planning.

Donor pathways

Donor egg and donor sperm programmes

Thoughtful, transparent counselling for patients evaluating donor options after self-cycle discussion has been fully explored.

Clinical quality

IVF governance and standardisation

National-level work in centre strategy, quality frameworks, staff training, and treatment consistency across IVF systems.

Human support

Compassionate counselling

Patients receive clear explanations, emotionally safe conversations, and structured next-step planning instead of vague reassurance.

Professional mentorship

Patients served

Mission & Philosophy

"To combine science, honesty, and empathy so every couple receives clarity, dignity, and the best possible chance at parenthood."

What patients need most

Fertility treatment is not only about success rates. It is also about informed choices, emotional safety, transparent communication, and care that respects how vulnerable the process can feel.

Who she serves

  • Couples struggling with infertility, including complex or repeat-failure cases
  • Women with endometriosis, PCOS, or age-related infertility
  • Patients exploring self IVF, donor IVF, or fertility preservation
  • IVF professionals and centres seeking training, structure, and strategic mentorship
