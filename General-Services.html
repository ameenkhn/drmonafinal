Clinical services

Key fertility services offered through Dr. Mona's practice

Assessment

Infertility assessment

History review, cycle analysis, ovarian reserve interpretation, semen factors, and a practical understanding of where the fertility challenge may be coming from.

Treatment

In-vitro fertilisation (IVF)

Evidence-based cycle planning with careful stimulation strategy, monitoring, embryo planning, and realistic communication around expectations.

Advanced ART

Intra cytoplasmic sperm injection (ICSI)

Targeted support for selected male-factor and advanced cases where ICSI offers a more appropriate fertilisation approach.

Surgical planning

Fertility-enhancing surgeries

When surgery is relevant to the fertility plan, treatment strategy is coordinated so procedures support pregnancy goals rather than delay them unnecessarily.

Genetic support

Preimplantation genetic testing (PGT)

Used in selected situations to improve embryo-level decision-making and reduce risk when there is a clear clinical reason to do so.

Preservation

Freezing: egg, sperm, and embryos

Useful for timing, medical reasons, and future planning when fertility preservation supports the patient's broader family-building choices.

Onco-fertility

Preservation for cancer patients

Urgent, time-sensitive fertility preservation planning for patients who need to protect reproductive options before cancer treatment begins.

Second opinion

IVF failure case review

Detailed review of previous cycles, embryo quality, lab parameters, and stimulation strategy with actionable next steps for patients who need a clearer direction.

Professional support

Training, strategy, and mentorship

IVF centre quality optimisation, governance frameworks, staff training, and leadership mentoring for clinics and fertility teams.

Cases that especially benefit from Dr. Mona's expertise

Where experienced pattern recognition matters most

Not every difficult case needs another cycle. Some need a better read.

Dr. Mona's value is strongest when fertility treatment has become repetitive, contradictory, or time-sensitive. The objective is not to add more interventions, but to identify what the case is truly asking for next.

01

Repeated IVF failures

When previous cycles have failed, the priority becomes identifying pattern errors in stimulation, embryo quality, transfer design, or timing instead of repeating the same pathway.

02

Recurrent implantation failure

Embryo grading, transfer technique, endometrial readiness, and cycle architecture need a systematic review so implantation is approached with more clinical precision.

03

Endometriosis-associated infertility

Disease severity, ovarian reserve, pain history, surgical impact, and IVF timing have to be balanced together rather than treated as isolated variables.

04

PCOS and age-related infertility

Ovulation patterns, metabolic factors, ovarian response, and the urgency created by age have to be combined into a plan that is realistic about timing and prognosis.

05

Self IVF versus donor decision-making

Ethical, transparent counselling is essential when patients need help understanding whether a self-cycle remains reasonable or donor options are more likely to succeed.

06

International and remote second opinions

Especially valuable for patients who want a credible plan review before travelling, changing clinics, or committing resources to another expensive IVF cycle.
